My Story

I’ve always been obsessed with astrophotography, and back in 2021 I discovered the thrill of flight-transit shots—those magical instants when a plane crosses in front of a celestial body. I would spend hours glued to flight trackers, scanning the skies for any hint of a glinting light heading toward the Sun or hearing a distant plane engine rumble. Every time I sensed a possible transit, I’d dash outside with my camera, hoping to nail the perfect shot.

That obsession led me to record flight and celestial data long before the app existed. By logging countless hours of flight paths, timestamps, speeds, and positions, I built a solid database to test predictions and validate the trigonometry that drives the app’s accuracy.

Mission

My goal is to make transit photography more accessible and rewarding. We combine real-time flight data and precise celestial calculations to predict upcoming transits with a configurable lead time and margin of error. While the app is not 100% perfect—nature and real-world variables always have the final say—it helps you spend less time crunching numbers and more time behind the lens.

Our Journey

From that trove of pre-app data, I distilled the key trigonometric relationships needed to forecast transits. Then, with the help of OpenAI’s o4-mini-high model, I translated those equations into clean, efficient code—no genius required, just a passion for both astronomy and aviation. Every day, I dedicate time to refining the algorithms, improving the UI, and incorporating feedback from our growing community of transit photographers.
